RICE PROCESSING

The harvested rice kernel, known as paddy, or rough, rice, is enclosed by the hull, or husk. Milling usually removes both the hull and bran layers of the kernel, and a coating of glucose and talc is sometimes applied to give the kernel a glossy finish..

HARVEST

The controlled germination of cereal grains that results in malt is initiated by adding moisture and is arrested by removing the moisture before the young plant grows out of its seed covering. The malting process itself consists of three stages: steeping, germination, and kilning. In steeping, the grain is placed in a tank with water and absorbs moisture, awakening the embryo within the kernel.

DISEASE

Most rice diseases can be managed to minimize losses. The four most important strategies for rice disease management are to rotate crops, plant resistant varieties, plant in warm soil and use fungicides when necessary. An integrated approach that uses all of these methods is the most effective and profitable.

Pesticide use in rice farming is a common practice in the Mekong Delta and poses ecological risks to aquatic organisms, the environment, and human health.
